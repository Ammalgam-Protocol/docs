# Security at Ammalgam

Ammalgam unifies lending, borrowing, and trading inside a single liquidity
system. That design unlocks capital efficiency, but it also concentrates
complexity that simpler lending markets and AMMs never have to reason about.
We treated security as an ongoing, layered process rather than a one-time
milestone. Every review was set up to challenge the protocol from a different
angle and shrink the space of unknowns before anyone deposits a dollar.

## A layered review process

### Independent audits

Our first reviews came from **ChainSecurity** and **0xMacro**, while the
protocol was still taking shape. They surfaced issues across the core of the
system. Some required redesigns, and others challenged assumptions that looked
reasonable until an outside expert pulled on them. That work reshaped parts of
our financial logic, liquidation architecture, and accounting, and became part
of the foundation the final design is built on.

### Expanding the review surface

ChainSecurity returned for a second review, and we opened the same code to a
**Cantina audit competition**, a format where a large field of independent
researchers hunt for bugs in parallel, each bringing different backgrounds and
methods. Breadth matters here. Some findings overlapped with earlier reviews,
but many did not, which reinforced a lesson we saw throughout development: every
layer of review finds something the previous layers missed.

### Formal verification, manual, and AI review

For the most complex parts of the protocol we engaged a formal verification
team, applying invariant testing, fuzzing, and mathematical proofs to validate
key properties across a wide range of conditions. We added further manual
review from a top-ranked researcher from the competition, and expanded our use
of AI-assisted security tooling. AI scanners are noisy, and many findings turn
out to be duplicates or false positives. Even so, the high false-positive rate
repeatedly forced us to recheck our assumptions and confirm the code behaves
exactly as intended, and the findings that survived that scrutiny were among
the most thorough we saw from any single method.

### Reviewing the fixes

Finding issues is only half the job. The other half is making sure the fixes
don't introduce new ones. A final stage re-examined every remediation and the
new assumptions created while fixing earlier findings, challenging those
changes the same way the original code had been challenged.

## Beyond the smart contract

Many of the most serious incidents in crypto never touched a contract bug. They
came from compromised credentials, social engineering, infrastructure failures,
or operational mistakes. As confidence in the codebase has grown, our attention
has shifted toward operational security: incident-response planning, hardened
internal processes, and participation in industry initiatives such as
**SEAL 911**.

## Balancing safety and decentralization

We believe in immutability, and our long-term goal is to remove administrative
control entirely. But launching a fully immutable lending protocol on day one
carries its own risks. Real users and real markets expose assumptions that no
test environment can. For that reason Ammalgam currently keeps a small set of
safeguards built strictly around user protection:

- **A 7-day timelock** on protocol upgrades, so changes are visible well before
  they take effect.
- **A preconfigured emergency pause**, so activity can be halted immediately if
  a serious issue is found.

These mechanisms exist to provide time and protection. They give no arbitrary
control over user funds or protocol state, and we intend to wind them down as
the protocol proves itself under real conditions.

## Looking forward

Security in DeFi is no longer just about smart-contract bugs. It now spans
protocol design, market structure, operational security, governance, and human
behavior. We've approached it as a layered process across all of these, knowing
that we have to find and fix every bug while an attacker needs only one. No set
of measures guarantees perfection, but each layer increases the confidence that
the protocol has been challenged from every angle before your capital is
inside, and we'll keep investing in that as tools and threats evolve.


## Trusted reviewers

The protocol has been examined by established audit firms and a competitive
field of independent researchers, alongside formal verification and
AI-assisted analysis.
